About the 306A Red Seal Exam — Sheet Metal Worker

The 306A Sheet Metal Worker Red Seal exam is Canada's national certification for sheet metal tradespeople working in HVAC ductwork fabrication and installation, architectural sheet metal, industrial exhaust systems, and commercial building envelope work. Red Seal 306A certification is recognized in all provinces and territories.

The exam consists of approximately 100 multiple-choice questions based on the current Red Seal Occupational Standard, requiring a minimum score of 70%. Exam time is 3 hours. The exam tests hands-on trade knowledge, not just code recall — expect questions requiring calculation and layout reasoning.

Topic Breakdown (306A RSOS)

  • Pattern Development (~20%) — The most heavily tested topic. Parallel line development (for rectangular ducts, cylinders), radial line development (for cones, pyramids), and triangulation (for transitions between different shapes). True length determination and gore construction are regularly tested. Understanding WHEN to apply each method is as important as knowing HOW.
  • Duct Construction & SMACNA Standards (~18%) — Pressure class classification (1/2" to 10" WG), gauge selection by pressure class and duct size, joint reinforcement (T-bars, angles), support spacing per SMACNA, duct sealing classes (A — all seams; B — transverse joints; C — no requirement), and leakage classification.
  • Seams, Joints & Connections (~16%) — Pittsburgh lock, grooved seam, drive cleats, S-cleats, standing seam, snap-lock, raw edge, and their applications. Knowing which seam is appropriate for which pressure class and duct orientation is essential.
  • Architectural Sheet Metal (~14%) — Roof flashings, gutters and downspouts, copings, sills, and expansion joints. Slope requirements for gutters (1/8" per foot minimum), gutter sizing based on roof drainage area, and thermal expansion allowances for metal in exposed environments.
  • Materials & Fabrication (~16%) — Galvanized steel, stainless steel, aluminum, copper, and specialty coatings. Gauge to thickness conversion, material compatibility (galvanic series), forming operations (brakes, slitters, plasma cutting), and equipment safety.
  • Installation & Safety (~16%) — Fall protection, rigging, hand and power tool safety, scaffold requirements, WHMIS/GHS, and site-specific safety programs. Sheet metal installation practices for mechanical rooms, plenums, and exposed architectural work.

Exam Strategy for 306A

Pattern development is your most valuable study area. At 20% of the exam, it's where candidates lose or gain the most marks. Practice constructing all three pattern types from scratch. Draw out the development for a 45° offset rectangular-to-round transition using triangulation — if you can do that, you can handle any exam question on layout.

Memorize the SMACNA pressure class table. Every duct construction decision (gauge, reinforcement, support, seam type) flows from the pressure class. Know the six classes (½, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 10" WC), when each is used, and how positive vs. negative pressure affects construction requirements.

Seam selection is applied knowledge. The exam will describe a duct condition (round, rectangular, positive pressure, insulated, architectural) and ask which seam is appropriate. Build a mental matrix: Pittsburgh = rectangular duct longitudinal seam; grooved = round spiral; standing seam = architectural; drive/S cleat = transverse connection.

Key Reference: Pattern Development Methods

  • Parallel Line Development: Used for prisms (rectangular ducts) and cylinders (round ducts). Lines in development are parallel — equal spacing along the perimeter.
  • Radial Line Development: Used for pyramids and cones (tapered fittings). Lines radiate from an apex point. Requires finding the true length of the slant height.
  • Triangulation: Used for transition pieces connecting different shapes (rectangle-to-round, offset transitions). Divide surface into triangles, find true length of each diagonal, then unfold.
