About the 276A Red Seal Exam — Welder

The 276A Welder Red Seal exam is Canada's national certification for welders who work across industrial, construction, pipeline, and manufacturing sectors. A Red Seal 276A endorsement validates your knowledge of all major welding processes, blueprint reading, metallurgy, and welding standards — and allows you to work anywhere in Canada without provincial re-examination.

The exam consists of 120 multiple-choice questions based on the 2023 Red Seal Occupational Standard (RSOS), with a passing score of 70%. Exam time is 3 hours.

Topic Breakdown (2023 RSOS)

  • SMAW — Shielded Metal Arc Welding (~20%) — Electrode classification (E7018, E6010, E6011), polarity selection (DCEP/DCEN), amperage settings per electrode diameter, striking an arc, weaving patterns, and identifying weld discontinuities. The most heavily tested process.
  • GMAW — Gas Metal Arc Welding (~18%) — Short-circuit, spray, and pulse transfer modes, wire feed speed vs. voltage relationships, shielding gas selection (C25, 100% Argon, C10), contact tip-to-work distance, and GMAW-P (pulsed GMAW) for thin materials.
  • GTAW — Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (~15%) — AC vs. DC polarity for aluminum vs. steel, tungsten electrode preparation and contamination, filler rod selection (ER70S-2, ER308L for stainless), back-purging requirements, and high-frequency start.
  • FCAW — Flux-Cored Arc Welding (~12%) — Self-shielded vs. gas-shielded FCAW, E71T-1 vs. E71T-8 electrode selection, porosity causes, out-of-position capabilities, and weld quality in structural applications.
  • Welding Theory, Metallurgy & Distortion (~15%) — Heat input calculations, pre-heat and interpass temperature requirements, hardenability and carbon equivalent (CE), HAZ characteristics, and distortion control techniques (backstep, balanced welding).
  • Safety, Codes & Symbols (~10%) — CSA W47.1, CSA W59, CWB certification categories, blueprint reading and weld symbols (AWS A2.4), PPE requirements, fire watch, confined space, and fume extraction.
  • OFC — Oxyfuel Cutting (~10%) — Tip selection, pressures, kerf width, and cutting speed for different steel thicknesses. Plasma arc cutting (PAC) basics also appear.

Exam Strategy for 276A

Theory, not technique. The Red Seal exam tests your understanding of WHY — why you choose a specific polarity, why porosity occurs, why pre-heat is required — not just your ability to weld. Strong candidates who may not be the best welder in the shop often outscore skilled welders who haven't studied the theory.

Master weld symbols. AWS A2.4 weld symbols appear on nearly every 276A exam. Practice interpreting groove welds, fillet welds, back/backing welds, and surfacing symbols from a print.

Know your discontinuities. The ability to identify discontinuity causes (porosity, undercut, incomplete fusion, hot cracks, cold cracks) and link them to process parameters is heavily tested.
